“John the Eagle” Giovanni Riggi (born 1925) is a New Jersey mobster who has been a member of the DeCavalcante criminal family since the 1940s, when the organisation was still known by its original name. When he was a Caporegime, Riggi was the commander of the family’s “Elizabeth crew.” During the 1970s, he was the acting boss, and from roughly 1980, he has been the official boss. Riggi was held in the Federal Medical Center (FMC) in Devens, Massachusetts, after being convicted of extortion and labour racketeering. On November 27, 2012, he was freed. Riggi is said to be the inspiration for Ercole DiMeo, a named but unseen character from HBO’s The Sopranos.
