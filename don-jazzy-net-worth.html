Don Jazzy: Nigerian Record Producer with $10 Million Net Worth

Don Jazzy, born in Umuahia, Abia State in November 1982, is a Nigerian record producer, singer-songwriter, musician, and former CEO. With a net worth of $10 million, he started singing and playing drums at four years old and bass guitar at 12.

He was the CEO of record label Mo’Hits Records and is currently the CEO of Mavin Records. He produced D’banj’s 2004 album No Long Thing in 2005 and has produced songs for various artists, including Dr SID, D’Prince, Wande Coal, Naeto C, Darey, Sauce Kid, Tiwa Savage, and Ikechukwu.

Jazzy won Producer of the Year at the 2006 Nigerian Music Awards and Music Producer of the Year at the 2007 Nigerian Entertainment Awards. He collaborated with Kanye West and Jay-Z as part of the album Watch the Throne, released in 2011. Forbes rated him the 36th most powerful celebrity in Africa. He was also part of Samsung’s Built for Africa products featuring an Afro-Pop home theater.
